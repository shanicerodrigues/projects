100 Years of Wildfires
in California

Interactive timeseries map dashboard in Tableau

     Dynamic phenomenon remains difficult to describe in a single frame map, however there are a variety of applications that can create multi-frame or animated maps. Today, I will be using Tableau to create an interactive map dashboard, where a variety of symbols, spatial and attribute data can be used to describe phenomena, even across several decades. The subject of interest will be wildfires in California, where documentation goes back to 100 years ago! As a bit of background, California has had a number of policy changes in fire control that has contributed to major alterations in wildfire regimes. In 1926, the United States Forest Service (USFS) implemented a severe fire suppression policy, requiring fires of 10 acres or less to be put out, and then in 1935, for all fires to be put out by 10 A.M. the next day. Continental fire suppression had allowed natural fire fuels, such as forest debris, to build up and resulted in massive, uncontrollable fires in the late 1900 to the present day as shown in the bottom bar graph of the map dashboard. In the upper right of the dashboard, the year can be changed to show the fires in that time period, which can be hovered over to show the fire name, cause, life length, and acres burned. The larger the symbol on the map, the more acres burned by that fire. Additionally, the graph on the left shows the monthly number of fires by cause, such as by lightening, campfire or arson.
